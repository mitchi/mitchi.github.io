Additional Consideration:

For thrown objects, if gravity increases velocity, the Dungeon Master (DM) may apply an additional 25% damage, at their discretion.

Handling Large Creatures and Objects

A character's Strength (STR) determines the size of creatures and objects they can manipulate, push, or throw. Pushing is generally easier than throwing.

Strength vs. Size

Strength Score Maximum Size Manipulable
1-5 Small
6-10 Medium
11-20 Large
21-40 Huge
41-80 Gargantuan
81+ Colossal

Key Examples:

  • Strength 1-5: Can push Medium creatures, throw Small ones.
  • Strength 6-10: Can handle and throw Medium creatures.
  • Strength 70: Can push Colossal creatures but not throw them.
  • Strength 90: Can both push and throw Colossal creatures.

Throwing Distance Calculation

Throwing distance depends on the size of the object and the thrower’s Strength. Larger creatures are harder to throw, while higher Strength allows for greater distances. A minimum Strength is required to throw creatures of larger sizes.

Formula:

The formula to calculate throw distance is: D = 15 \times \left( \frac{S - S_{\text{min}}}{C} \right) Where:

  • D = distance (in feet),
  • S = character’s Strength,
  • S_{\text{min}} = minimum Strength required to throw the creature,
  • C = creature’s size category (e.g., Colossal = 6).

Practical Example:

A barbarian with Strength 10 can throw a Small creature 135 feet, enough to help an ally cross a chasm by attaching a rope. A character with Strength 90 can throw a Colossal creature 22.5 feet—perfect for hurling massive threats in battle!
